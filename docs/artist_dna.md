# Artist DNA Profiles
## Music Co-Pilot Knowledge Base
### Used by: Theory Agent, Production Agent, Teaching Agent, Sound Engineering Agent

This document profiles 12 artists in terms of their signature chord progressions, production techniques, drum patterns, sound design approach, and key reference tracks. When a user references one of these artists, use this as grounding context for suggestions.

---

## 1. Massive Attack
**Genre**: Trip-hop, dark electronic, cinematic
**Key**: Heavy minor keys, Phrygian coloring, drone-based harmony
**BPM range**: 70–110, often half-time feel

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Extreme harmonic minimalism — "Angel" uses only 2 chords (Cm and C major) for 6+ minutes and never gets boring
- C Phrygian is a home base — the ♭2 creates that dark, suspended tension
- Pedal point is a primary compositional tool: keep one bass note constant while melody and harmony shift above it
- Unresolved progressions — chords that loop without landing create the "suspended haze" feel
- Modal ambiguity between major and minor versions of the same root

**Signature Progressions**:
- i–I shuttle (Cm–C): bimodal flicker, neither fully dark nor bright
- Single chord drone with melodic movement above (Glory Box-adjacent technique)
- Slow descending basslines under static harmony

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Sample-based percussion — "Angel" uses bongo loops from The Incredible Bongo Band, heavily processed
- Tribal, relentless rhythm underneath ambient layers
- Dub-influenced: heavy reverb on snare, almost completely dry kick and sidestick
- Half-time or trip-hop groove: kick on 1, snare on 3, floating hi-hats

**Production Techniques**:
- Two basslines running simultaneously — one dirty, one clean — the fat comes from managing both
- Guitars used as "buzzy animated pads" — thin-sounding, almost DI, crunchy but not present
- Crushed spring reverb on snare with long pre-delay
- Everything wet except the low end drums and sidestick — stark contrast
- Vocals soaked in reverb for claustrophobic, slithering effect
- Layers added and removed dramatically across a 6+ minute track

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Angel" — 2-chord Phrygian drone, two basslines, tribal percussion
- "Teardrop" — A Mixolydian, 77 BPM, music box melody over Rhodes
- "Unfinished Sympathy" — lush orchestration over trip-hop groove
- "Safe From Harm" — deep sub bass, ominous atmosphere

**Spliced Aesthetic**: Deep, heavy sub-bass with melodic fragility above it. Dark but never aggressive.

## 2. Portishead
**Genre**: Trip-hop, cinematic, dark soul
**Key**: Minor keys with jazz chromaticism, chromatic voice leading
**BPM range**: 60–95

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- "Glory Box" is the masterclass: chromatic descending bassline under held top notes — the bass walks away while the melody stays still, like "two people watching one walk away"
- Half-diminished chords (m7♭5) create modal ambiguity and jazz-influenced tension
- Borrowed chords from parallel modes — C-7(♭5) borrowed from E♭ Dorian in Glory Box
- Chromatic passing chords connect diatonic chords — non-diatonic chord as accidental interchange responding to bass motion
- Chorus of Glory Box: Dm → Dm/C → B♭m7♭5 → BMaj7 — beautiful dissonance built from one chromatic bass motion

**Signature Progressions**:
- i → i/VII → ♭VI → V(maj7): chromatic descending bass with static top note
- Dm → Dm/C → Bm7♭5 → BMaj7 (Glory Box chorus)
- Slow ii–V–I with half-diminished substitutions

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Sample-based with vinyl crackle and texture
- Half-time feel at slow tempos
- Looped breakbeats — organic, imperfect timing adds human feel
- Sparse: space between hits is as important as the hits

**Production Techniques**:
- Vinyl samples as atmospheric texture, not just rhythm
- Theremin-like swells for ethereal texture
- Deep, resonant bassline creating dread from the first note
- Analog imperfections deliberately preserved — aversion to digital sterility
- Guitar: bluesy, distorted, sitting in the atmosphere rather than leading

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Glory Box" — chromatic voice leading masterclass, slow half-time, 60 BPM
- "Sour Times" — C# minor, F#m7–Emaj7–D#7 chorus (II–I–VII, rare and distinctive)
- "Roads" — Em9 drone, vast space

**Splice Aesthetic**: Vinyl-textured breakbeat, spacious snare, deep bass, no unnecessary notes.

## 3. Skrillex
**Genre**: Dubstep (original UK-adjacent), brostep, electronic bass, experimental
**Key**: Minor keys, heavy modal coloring
**BPM range**: 140–150 (dubstep half-time), 128 (house contexts)

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Chord progressions are secondary to bass design — the progression sets the key, the bass IS the melody
- Minor key, often simple i–VI–III–VII or i–v loops
- Harmonic movement in the intro/breakdown, then stripped to bass motion in the drop
- "Supersonic" — bass house context, punchy FM lead over minor key loop

**Signature Bass Design** (core of the DNA):
- FM synthesis at the heart — FM8 is his most-used synth, Operator in Ableton is close
- "Modern Talking" wavetable in Massive + LFO on wavetable position = the talking/vowel bass
- 3-layer approach: sub (sine, 30–150 Hz) + mid growl (FM/wavetable, 150–3500 Hz) + hi fizz (3500+ Hz)
- Each layer gets different modulated effects: chorus, delay, ringmod, phaser, flanger, distortion
- Aggressive resampling chain: design → process with effects → render to audio → reimport → warp/edit → repeat
- Neurofunk "Reese notching" technique: filter notches carved into the bass at specific frequencies
- Vocal chops with heavy pitch shifting and formant processing

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Dubstep half-time: kick on 1, massive snare on 3
- Complex syncopated percussion between the main hits
- Layered percussion: multiple kick layers, layered snares, textural fills
- Heavy sidechain on everything to the kick

**Production Techniques**:
- Ableton Live as primary DAW
- FM8 + Massive + Serum for bass and lead design
- Aggressive sidechain compression
- Build-up: white noise sweep + snare roll + pitch bend up
- Drop: all dynamics released simultaneously

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites" — defining brostep template
- "Supersonic" (feat. Noisia, josh pan, Dylan Brady) — bass house, FM lead
- "Tears" (feat. Joker, Sleepnet) — UK dubstep influenced, dark melodic line
- "Rumble" (with Fred again..) — purest pre-brostep dubstep on Quest for Fire

## 4. Fred Again..
**Genre**: UK garage-influenced dance, emotional house, deep house
**Key**: Primarily major keys (~80% of output), simple diatonic harmony
**BPM range**: 120–135

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Radical simplicity: fewer chords and chord changes than the average pop hit
- Over 90% of tracks are completely in key — no modal interchange, no secondary dominants
- Fewer harmonic rhythm changes than peers — chords breathe for longer
- Emotional weight comes from melody and vocal samples layered over simple harmony, not from complex chord changes
- "Delilah (pull me out of this)" — B♭ Major, simple diatonic
- "Kammy (like i do)" — G minor, simple minor diatonic

**Signature Progressions**:
- I–V–vi–IV in major: warmth and accessibility
- i–VI–III–VII in minor: emotional minor loop
- Simple 2-chord loops held for extended periods

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Minimal but tight — never cluttered, always serves the emotion
- Groovy with swing — not robotic
- Kick, snare, hi-hats as the foundation with light percussion layers
- Beat doesn't overshadow the emotion of the melody

**Production Techniques**:
- Logic Pro as primary DAW
- Serum for lead sounds — warm basses, pads, plucks
- Avoid over-quantization — keep human feel (50% quantization or manual timing)
- Vocal sample manipulation: pitch shifting, time stretching, reverb, delay — voices become instruments
- Personal inspiration as foundation: specific real moments, real people, real emotions
- Space is as important as sound — don't overfill the mix
- Automation of filters and phasers to create constant evolution

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Marea (We've Lost Dancing)" (feat. The Blessed Madonna) — G# major, 8-bar progression, staccato riff
- "Delilah (pull me out of this)" — B♭ major, emotional house
- "Rumble" (with Skrillex) — rare collaboration showing his range into bass music

## 5. Ben Böhmer
**Genre**: Melodic house, progressive house, Anjunadeep-style
**Key**: Minor keys predominantly, extended voicings (7ths, 9ths, sus2)
**BPM range**: 122–126

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Start in minor key (A minor is a go-to)
- Longer progressions for more emotional depth — doesn't rush through changes
- Resolve back to root for continuity and grounding
- Chord extensions add warmth: 7ths, 9ths, sus2, add9
- Distribute chord notes across multiple instruments (not all stacked on one) — frequency clarity
- Am9 – Fmaj7 – Cadd9 – Gsus2 is a representative progression
- Often 2–3 chord tracks giving harmonic freedom

**Signature Progressions**:
- i – VI – III – VII with extended voicings: Am9 – Fmaj7 – Cadd9 – Gsus2
- Modal shifts within minor context
- Slow harmonic rhythm: one chord per 2–4 bars, breathe in the harmony

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Warm, round kicks — not punchy, rounded
- Low toms for depth and rhythm
- Shakers and rides for organic feel — stereo hi-hats with random panning
- Organic, human-feeling rhythms — uses loops rather than programmed grids
- Layered claps with reverb

**Production Techniques**:
- Ableton Live (Session View heavily for live performance)
- Serum or Diva for lead sounds — warm sawtooth and sine waves, subtle modulation
- "Horn-style" pads for atmosphere
- Fast mono plucks for brightness
- Long, dreamy pitched glide lead synth (Serum or Diva)
- Fifth bass technique: root note + fifth interval above for harmonic richness
- Sustained pitched Moog bass with glide in leads and arps
- Gradual filter automation: start filtered-down, open up through arrangement
- Arpeggiator on chords for movement without extra notes

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Beyond Beliefs" — 124 BPM reference track for melodic house structure
- "Father Ocean" remix (Monolink) — Father Ocean style, E minor
- "Vale" — E minor, Anjunadeep style

## 6. Hooverphonics
**Genre**: Dream pop, baroque pop, cinematic electronic
**Key**: Minor keys with lush orchestration, jazz-influenced voicings
**BPM range**: 60–90

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Lush, cinematic harmony — chords feel composed, not looped
- Jazz-influenced extended voicings: maj7, min7, add9
- "Mad About You" — melancholic, theatrical, minor key with orchestral sweep
- Slow harmonic rhythm, each chord given space to breathe and emote
- Strings and brass used as harmonic pad layers

**Signature Progressions**:
- i – VI – III – VII with orchestral voicing
- Classical cadences with electronic production underneath
- Unexpected chord color: borrowed chords from parallel major adding brightness to otherwise dark material

**Production Techniques**:
- Orchestral and string arrangements are central (not decorative)
- Voice is the anchor — everything else serves the vocal
- Cinematic reverb and space — large hall, long decay
- Electronic beats underneath organic orchestration

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Mad About You" — dark, sweeping, orchestral trip-hop

## 7. Deftones
**Genre**: Alternative metal, shoegaze, dream metal, post-hardcore
**Key**: Minor keys, Phrygian, Eb minor, power chord-driven with atmospheric pads
**BPM range**: 90–130

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Power chords as the foundation — root + fifth, no third
- Modal ambiguity — often hovering between Phrygian and Aeolian
- Diamond Eyes: B – A♯sus2 verse, A♯m – Fm chorus (Bb minor key, 108 BPM)
- Deceptive, "wolf in sheep's clothing" songwriting: commercially accessible surface with deeply unusual harmonic structure underneath
- CMND/CNTRL: descending chord progression with a bar removed every other repetition — rhythmic disorientation through harmonic means
- Shoegaze pads layered with metal riffing — textural contrast is the signature

**Signature Progressions**:
- Bb – Bbm – Ab – Bbm (Diamond Eyes)
- Ebm7 – Gm (Knife Party) with chromatic bass passing tones
- Power chords with Phrygian ♭2 passing motion

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Standard rock kit but with unusual time signatures and bar lengths
- Snare on 2 and 4 but with shifting patterns
- Shifting time signatures as compositional device (March of the Pigs: 7/8 influence)

**Production Techniques**:
- Terry Date as long-time producer — tight, focused mix
- Chino Moreno's voice: ethereal falsetto over dense guitars creates maximum contrast
- Guitars: dense, drop-tuned, but with pad-like sustain patches layered beneath
- Shoegaze wash underneath metal attack

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Diamond Eyes" — Bb minor, 108 BPM, prog-accessible metal
- "Knife Party" — Ebm7 / Gm, chromatic bass
- "Change (In the House of Flies)" — dreamlike, slow-burn

## 8. Nine Inch Nails / Trent Reznor
**Genre**: Industrial rock, industrial electronic, dark ambient
**Key**: Minor keys — Aeolian, Phrygian, Dorian, Minor Blues
**BPM range**: 90–140

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Minimalism as power: "Closer" uses only Cm7 and F for the entire track — 2 chords, maximum atmosphere
- Tritone relationships between adjacent chords: Dm → A♭ creates that quasi-evil quality (tritone apart)
- Chromatic chord movement: "Shit Mirror" uses A major → F major → E♭, completely unrelated chords that simply feel right
- Dorian-to-Natural-Minor shifts as a recurring motif: "09 Ghosts I" and "Beside You In Time" both use this trick
- Modal interchange between parallel major and minor: D major → D minor shifting within a section
- Repetitive, hypnotic patterns — the groove IS the progression

**Signature Progressions**:
- Cm7 – F (Closer: minimal, industrial)
- Dm – A♭ – G – Fm (March of the Pigs: tritone relationship)
- i – ♭VI – i (constant minor with dark neighbor chord)
- Natural minor → Dorian shift for emotional lift within darkness

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Roland R-70 drum machine (Closer snare)
- Kick sampled from "Nightclubbing" by Iggy Pop (Closer)
- Heavy electronic processing on all drum sounds
- Hard, mechanical, industrial feel — not humanized

**Production Techniques**:
- FM synthesis with Oberheim OB-Mx prototype (Closer bassline)
- Heavy distortion and noise processing on all sources
- Reversed and processed samples buried in the mix
- Layers continuously added throughout, dropped suddenly
- Simple chromatic piano melody endings for tension release
- Prototype synths and unusual hardware signal chain

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Closer" — Cm7/F, industrial minimalism, 2-chord loop
- "Hurt" — Bm, D, E verse; Bm – G – D – A chorus; borrowed chords
- "March of the Pigs" — tritone-adjacent chords, shifting time signature

## 9. CloZee
**Genre**: World bass, UK bass, glitch, dubstep, experimental electronic
**Key**: Minor keys with world music modal coloring
**BPM range**: 140 (dubstep half-time) to 85 (downtempo)

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Classical guitar background informs melodic approach — melody-first composition
- Freeform melodic lines over minor key harmony
- World music scales and intervals for exotic coloring
- "Courage" — downtempo, blissful synths + airy vocals + orchestral arrangements
- The bass IS the lead voice — as important as the melody
- Dark minor progressions with pretty, floating melodic lines above

**Signature Progressions**:
- Minor key with world modal coloring (Phrygian, harmonic minor)
- Sparse harmonic changes — melody carries the movement
- Long sustained chords underneath complex melodic lines

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Bass music half-time: massive drop energy
- World percussion elements layered with electronic drums
- Sub-bass felt as physical experience — working closely with subpac/sub monitoring

**Production Techniques**:
- FL Studio as primary DAW
- Sound design: classical guitar sensibility translated into electronic textures
- Sub-bass is central to composition, not an afterthought
- Blends world instruments (koto, ethnic strings) with electronic processing
- "Courage": blissful synths + airy vocals + epic orchestral arrangements

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Koto" — breakout single, Asian-influenced melody over electronic bass
- "Courage" (feat. ROZET) — downtempo, melodic, orchestral
- "Evasion" album — world bass debut

## 10. GRiZ
**Genre**: Future funk, electro-soul, electronic funk, bass music
**Key**: Major keys and Mixolydian, funk-influenced
**BPM range**: 95–128

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Funk and soul harmony: dominant 7ths, 9ths, 13ths
- Mixolydian ♭VII progressions for that funky major-but-bluesy feel
- Horn section voicings — chord stabs in the midrange
- Live saxophone integrated with electronic production

**Signature Progressions**:
- I7 – IV7 – I7 (funk blues base)
- Mixolydian: I – ♭VII – IV
- Extended jazz-funk voicings: 9ths and 13ths on dominant chords

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Four-on-the-floor with funk syncopation
- Heavy groove — hi-hat 16th note patterns with swing
- Layered percussion: shakers, congas, live feel

**Production Techniques**:
- Live saxophone performance integrated with electronic production
- Vintage-sounding synth bass
- Warm, analog-adjacent mix aesthetic

## 11. Deadmau5
**Genre**: Progressive house, techno, minimal electronic
**Key**: Minor keys predominantly, minimal harmonic movement
**BPM range**: 126–132

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Progressive: long, gradual builds over 8–16 bar sections
- Minimal harmonic movement — 2–4 chords held for extended periods
- Tension built through arrangement and texture, not through harmonic complexity
- Arpeggiated leads over sustained pad chords

**Signature Progressions**:
- i – VI – III – VII held for 8+ bars each
- Drone-like sustained chords with melodic arpeggio above
- Subtle voice leading between chords, no dramatic jumps

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Four-on-the-floor kick, tight 8th-note hi-hats
- Minimalist — less is more
- Subtle percussion automation creates movement without adding elements

**Production Techniques**:
- Ableton Live (has discussed workflow openly)
- Analog modeling synthesis for warm pads
- Progressive arrangement: elements enter and exit gradually
- Sidechain compression on pads to create pumping feel
- Long, gradual filter sweeps as primary movement tool

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Strobe" — the defining progressive house template
- "Some Chords" — minimal chord progression, maximum atmosphere

## 12. Sofi Tukker
**Genre**: Dance-pop, house, Brazilian-influenced electronic, world dance
**Key**: Mix of major and minor, bossa nova and Brazilian rhythmic influence
**BPM range**: 120–128

**Signature Chord Approach**:
- Brazilian bossa nova harmony: sophisticated but accessible
- Portuguese-language poetry as lyrical source (Drinkee adapted from Brazilian poet Chacal)
- "Drinkee" — A minor, high melodic complexity
- Fusion of driven beats with samba inflections
- Chord stabs as rhythmic percussion element (bossa nova influence)

**Signature Progressions**:
- Minor key bossa-influenced: Am – Dm – G7 – Am with chromatic passing chords
- Rhythmic chord stabs on off-beats (bossa skank equivalent)
- Polyrhythmic layering: cowbells, castanets, bongos against electronic beats

**Drum/Rhythm Signature**:
- Polyrhythmic: bossa nova rhythms layered with house kick
- Cowbells, congas, castanets, bongos — world percussion over electronic beats
- Custom Ableton drum racks with MIDI triggers for live performance

**Production Techniques**:
- Ableton Live for live performance with extensive custom drum racks
- Sophie plays live guitar, percussion
- Clean, bold production — vibrant synths, punchy percussion
- Multilingual lyrics as a texture element
- Community-building built into the live experience ("Freak Fam")

**Key Reference Tracks**:
- "Drinkee" — A minor, bossa-house fusion, Grammy-nominated
- "Best Friend" — featured in Apple iPhone X launch
- "Purple Hat" — dance floor anthem

## Cross-Reference: "Artists Known For Dark + Pretty Melodic Line" (Your Core Reference Cluster)

These artists all share the combination you described — dark production, heavy bass, with a fragile or beautiful melodic line above:

| Artist | Darkness Source | Melodic Beauty Source |
|---|---|---|
| Massive Attack | Phrygian drone, sub bass, industrial guitars | Horace Andy's ethereal falsetto, music box leads |
| Portishead | Chromatic voice leading, half-diminished chords | Beth Gibbons' raw vocal, vinyl texture |
| Deftones | Drop-tuned power chords, Phrygian | Chino's falsetto, shoegaze pad wash |
| NIN | Tritone relationships, industrial drum machines | Melodic synth leads, chromatic piano |
| CloZee | World bass drops, heavy sub | Classical guitar-influenced melodic lines |
| Ben Böhmer | Dark minor key harmony | Dreamy lead synth, gliding arpeggios |
| Two Feet | Dark minor groove | Intimate, raw vocal over heavy bass |

**The pattern**: Keep the bottom heavy and dark. Keep the top delicate and melodic. The contrast IS the sound.
