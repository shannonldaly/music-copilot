# Famous Chord Progressions Quick Reference
## Music Co-Pilot Knowledge Base
### Used by: Theory Agent, Teaching Agent

This document lists well-known named progressions with their emotional character, famous examples, and production notes. Use this when a user references a sound or a specific song to identify which named progression is the right starting point.

---

## Named Progressions by Emotional Character

### WARM / RESOLVED / HOPEFUL

**The Axis of Awesome (I–V–vi–IV)**
- C major: C – G – Am – F
- Songs: "Don't Stop Believin'", "Let It Be", "With or Without You", "Someone Like You"
- Character: Complete, universally satisfying, circular
- Producer note: Rotate to vi–IV–I–V for minor emotional weight with same chords

**The Pachelbel Canon (I–V–vi–iii–IV–I–IV–V)**
- D major: D – A – Bm – F#m – G – D – G – A
- Songs: "Memories" (Maroon 5), "Starships" (Nicki Minaj)
- Character: Cyclical, inevitable, warm nostalgia
- Producer note: The iii chord is underused — it creates motion without disruption

**The 50s Doo-Wop (I–vi–IV–V)**
- C major: C – Am – F – G
- Songs: "Stand By Me", "Blue Moon", "Earth Angel"
- Character: Nostalgic, innocent, clean
- Producer note: Apply lo-fi treatment to modernize

---

### MELANCHOLIC / BITTERSWEET

**The Royal Road (IV–V–iii–vi)**
- C major: F – G – Em – Am
- Songs: Common in anime soundtracks, J-pop, film scores
- Character: Reaching, nostalgic, just out of grasp
- Producer note: The iii chord (Em) is the secret ingredient

**The Sensitive Songwriter (I–V–vi–IV, specific voicing)**
- Same chords as Axis but with intimate acoustic framing
- Character: Confessional, introspective, personal
- Producer note: Voiced on acoustic guitar or intimate piano rather than pads

**The Dorian Minor (i–IV)**
- A Dorian: Am – D (major IV in a minor context)
- Songs: "Oye Como Va" (Santana), "So What" (Miles Davis)
- Character: Dark but with a major brightness that makes it groovy rather than sad
- Producer note: This is the Dorian signature — if you want minor that grooves, use Dorian

---

### DARK / TENSE / UNRESOLVED

**The Andalusian Cadence (i–♭VII–♭VI–V)**
- A minor: Am – G – F – E
- Songs: "Stairway to Heaven" (intro), "Hit the Road Jack", flamenco universally
- Character: Descending fatalism, inevitable, Spanish/dark
- Producer note: The E major (V major, not minor) is Phrygian dominant — adds exotic tension

**The Minor Plagal (i–iv)**
- A minor: Am – Dm
- Songs: Most common two-chord progression in trap and hip-hop
- Character: Cool, dark, circular, never resolves
- Producer note: This is the backbone of modern rap production — endlessly loop-able

**The NIN Drone (i7 – IV)**
- C minor: Cm7 – F
- Songs: "Closer" (Nine Inch Nails)
- Character: Industrial, hypnotic, all atmosphere
- Producer note: Two chords for an entire track. Production does the work.

---

### JAZZ / SOPHISTICATED

**The ii–V–I (Jazz Foundation)**
- C major: Dm7 – G7 – Cmaj7
- Character: Maximum resolution — the strongest cadence in Western music
- Producer note: In minor: Dm7♭5 – G7alt – Cm. Tritone sub: Dm7 – D♭7 – Cmaj7

**The Turnaround (I–vi–ii–V)**
- C major: Cmaj7 – Am7 – Dm7 – G7
- Character: Perpetual motion, jazz standard foundation
- Producer note: Repeat endlessly or use to set up a resolution

**The Circle of Fifths Fragment (V/V – V – I)**
- C major: D7 – G7 – C
- Character: Double-dominant weight, gospel influence
- Producer note: Chain longer: E7 – A7 – D7 – G7 – C for maximum forward motion

---

### CINEMATIC / DRAMATIC

**The Creep (I–III–IV–iv)**
- G major: G – B – C – Cm
- Songs: "Creep" (Radiohead)
- Character: The borrowed iv chord creates a devastating dark shift
- Producer note: The III major (B in G) before the iv is chromatic — unexpected brightness then sudden dark arrival

**The Neapolitan Move (i – ♭II – V – i)**
- A minor: Am – B♭ – E – Am
- Character: Operatic drama, maximum surprise
- Producer note: The ♭II (Neapolitan) chord is one half-step above tonic in major — extremely unexpected, very powerful

**The Coltrane Changes (I – ♭III – ♭VI – I)**
- C major: C – E♭ – A♭ – C
- Character: Maximum harmonic surprise through major thirds cycle
- Producer note: Advanced. Each chord center is a major third away. Disorienting but sophisticated.

---

### MODAL / EXOTIC

**The Phrygian Shuttle (i – ♭II)**
- E Phrygian: Em – F
- Character: Dark, Spanish, half-step tension above tonic
- Producer note: This is flamenco's core motion. Works in metal, dark electronic, film score.

**The Mixolydian Shuttle (I – ♭VII)**
- G Mixolydian: G – F
- Character: Major but bluesy — feels like classic rock or Celtic
- Producer note: The ♭VII is borrowed from parallel minor. Used in "Sweet Home Alabama", Celtic folk, reggae.

**The Lydian Drift (I – II)**
- C Lydian: C – D
- Character: Floating, ethereal, science-fiction
- Producer note: The raised 4th in Lydian makes the II chord major instead of minor — dreamy and uplifting without being saccharine. John Williams uses this constantly.

## Quick Emotional Tag Reference

| You want... | Start with... |
|---|---|
| Warm, circular, hopeful | I–V–vi–IV (Axis) |
| Dark and looping, never resolves | i–VI–III–VII |
| Descending, fatalistic | i–VII–VI–V (Andalusian) |
| Jazz sophistication | ii–V–I |
| Industrial, hypnotic | i7 – IV (just two chords) |
| Bittersweet nostalgia | IV–V–iii–vi (Royal Road) |
| Surprising dark shift | I–III–IV–iv (Creep) |
| Groovy minor | i–IV (Dorian shuttle) |
| Floating, ethereal | I–II (Lydian) |
| Spanish/exotic | i–♭II (Phrygian shuttle) |
| Maximum jazz resolution | ii–V–I |
| Classic rock/bluesy | I–♭VII (Mixolydian) |
| Chromatic voice leading descent | i – i/VII – ♭VI – V (Glory Box style) |
| Industrial two-chord drone | Cm7 – F (NIN style) |
| James Bond / line cliché | i – iM7 – i7 – i6 |
