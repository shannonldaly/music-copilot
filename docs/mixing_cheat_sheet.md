# Mixing Cheat Sheet — Music Elements in Ableton Live

A quick reference for EQ, compression, reverb, panning, and volume settings for 29 common instruments and sound sources. Used by the Sound Engineering Agent to give accurate, instrument-specific mixing guidance.

---

## 1. Acoustic Kick Drum
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 60–100 Hz (body), 2–4 kHz (attack)
- **Boost**: 60–100 Hz for weight; 2–4 kHz for attack presence
- **Cut**: Muddy frequencies around 200–300 Hz if needed
- **Panning**: Center
- **Volume**: Set to sit well with bass; typically around 0 dB or slightly lower
- **Compression**: Ratio 4:1 to 6:1; fast attack (~10 ms), release to control dynamics
- **Reverb**: Short decay (~200 ms), minimal to avoid washout; room or small hall with low mix

---

## 2. Percussions (Conga, Shakers, Tambourine)
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 100–800 Hz (body), up to 8 kHz (attack and shimmer)
- **Boost**: 3–8 kHz for attack and brightness
- **Cut**: Remove unnecessary low frequencies below 100 Hz
- **Panning**: Spread across stereo field for width
- **Volume**: Adjust for balance, often softer than main drums
- **Compression**: Light ratio (~2:1), moderate attack (~30 ms) for consistency
- **Reverb**: Short to medium decay (300–600 ms), add stereo spread

---

## 3. Synth Leads
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 1–4 kHz (presence), 100 Hz–1 kHz (body)
- **Boost**: 2–4 kHz for clarity, 8–12 kHz for air/shimmer
- **Cut**: Unnecessary low end below 100 Hz
- **Panning**: Center or slight stereo spread
- **Volume**: Prominent but balanced
- **Compression**: Light, ratio 2:1, slow attack (~30 ms) to sustain presence
- **Reverb**: Medium decay (700–1,200 ms), moderate mix for space

---

## 4. 808 Bass
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 20–100 Hz, with sub-bass emphasis
- **Boost**: 50–80 Hz for sub; 200–300 Hz for body
- **Cut**: Muddy frequencies around 200–300 Hz if needed
- **Panning**: Center
- **Volume**: Relative to kick for punch
- **Compression**: Light; use transient shaping if necessary
- **Reverb**: Usually minimal; if used, very short decay or slapback

---

## 5. Electronic Kick Drum
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 50–80 Hz (body), 2–4 kHz (attack)
- **Boost**: 50–80 Hz, 2–4 kHz
- **Cut**: Unnecessary low-end below 50 Hz if muddy
- **Panning**: Center
- **Volume**: Complementary to 808 bass
- **Compression**: Moderate ratio (~4:1), fast attack (~10 ms)
- **Reverb**: Very short or none; if used, short decay

---

## 6. Male & Female Lead Vocals
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 100 Hz–3 kHz
- **Boost**: 3–5 kHz for presence, 8–12 kHz for air
- **Cut**: Low frequencies below 80 Hz to reduce rumble
- **Panning**: Center
- **Volume**: Adjust to sit upfront
- **Compression**: Ratio 3:1 to 4:1; slow attack (~30 ms), release to maintain dynamics
- **Reverb**: Medium decay (600–1,000 ms), large hall or plate for lushness; adjust wet/dry

---

## 7. Backup Vocals (Male & Female, Rap & Singing)
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 100 Hz–8 kHz
- **Boost**: 3–6 kHz for clarity, 10–12 kHz for air
- **Cut**: Unnecessary low frequencies below 100 Hz
- **Panning**: Slightly spread for width
- **Volume**: Lower than lead, layered for texture
- **Compression**: 3:1 to 4:1; slow attack (~30 ms)
- **Reverb**: Medium decay, subtle to avoid washing out

---

## 8. Synth Pads
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 200 Hz–2 kHz (body), 8–12 kHz (air)
- **Boost**: 1–3 kHz for presence, 8–12 kHz for shimmer
- **Cut**: Unnecessary lows below 200 Hz to reduce muddiness
- **Panning**: Wide stereo spread
- **Volume**: Subtle, to add depth without overpowering
- **Compression**: Light, ratio 2:1; slow attack (~50 ms)
- **Reverb**: Large hall or lush reverb with long decay (1,200–2,000 ms)

---

## 9. Snare Drum
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 150–250 Hz (body), 2–4 kHz (attack), 5–8 kHz (brightness)
- **Boost**: 2–4 kHz for snap, 5–8 kHz for brightness
- **Cut**: Muddy frequencies around 200 Hz if needed
- **Panning**: Center
- **Volume**: Prominent but controlled
- **Compression**: Ratio 4:1, fast attack (~10 ms) to enhance snap
- **Reverb**: Medium decay (~400–600 ms), room or small hall

---

## 10. Claps
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 1–3 kHz
- **Boost**: 3–8 kHz for crispness
- **Cut**: Unnecessary lows below 200 Hz
- **Panning**: Center or slightly stereo
- **Volume**: Mix to sit behind the snare or layered with it
- **Reverb**: Short, with some stereo spread

---

## 11. Open and Closed Hi-Hats
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 8–12 kHz (attack), 12–15 kHz (shimmer)
- **Boost**: 10–12 kHz for shimmer
- **Cut**: Low frequencies below 200 Hz
- **Panning**: Open hats spread wide; closed hats centered or slightly off-center
- **Volume**: Varies per groove
- **Reverb**: Minimal; sometimes a very short slap delay

---

## 12. Cymbals (Crash, Ride)
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 300 Hz–10 kHz
- **Boost**: 8–12 kHz for shimmer
- **Cut**: Low frequencies below 300 Hz
- **Panning**: Wide stereo for crash; centered for ride
- **Volume**: Accentuate at key moments
- **Reverb**: Medium decay (600–1,200 ms), optional for wash

---

## 13. Toms
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 80–150 Hz (low toms), 150–250 Hz (high toms), 2–4 kHz (attack)
- **Boost**: 80–150 Hz for punch, 2–4 kHz for attack
- **Cut**: Unnecessary lows below 80 Hz
- **Panning**: Pan different toms across stereo field
- **Volume**: Dynamic, often layered
- **Reverb**: Short to medium decay (300–600 ms)

---

## 14. Bass Guitar
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 80–200 Hz
- **Boost**: 100–200 Hz for warmth; 700–900 Hz for attack
- **Cut**: Muddy frequencies around 300–500 Hz if needed
- **Panning**: Center
- **Volume**: Complementary to kick
- **Compression**: Ratio 4:1; moderate attack (~20 ms)
- **Reverb**: Usually minimal; if used, very short decay

---

## 15. Electric Guitar
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 80–1,000 Hz (body), 2–4 kHz (attack), 8–12 kHz (clarity)
- **Boost**: 2–4 kHz, 8–12 kHz for brightness
- **Cut**: Unnecessary lows below 80 Hz
- **Panning**: Hard left/right or stereo spread
- **Volume**: As needed for prominence
- **Reverb**: Medium decay (~600–1,000 ms), optional delay for space

---

## 16. Choir
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 150 Hz–2 kHz
- **Boost**: 1–3 kHz for presence
- **Cut**: Unnecessary lows below 150 Hz
- **Panning**: Wide stereo for lushness
- **Volume**: Blend with lead vocals
- **Reverb**: Large hall or cathedral reverb, long decay (~1,500 ms+)

---

## 17. Piano
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 27 Hz–4 kHz (low to high keys)
- **Boost**: 100–300 Hz for body, 3–5 kHz for attack, 8–12 kHz for brightness
- **Cut**: Unnecessary lows below 80 Hz (if not playing bass notes)
- **Panning**: Center or slightly spread for stereo feel
- **Volume**: Depends on context; often prominent but balanced
- **Reverb**: Medium to large hall (700–1,500 ms) for spaciousness

---

## 18. Electric Keys (Electric Piano, Rhodes, etc.)

- **Boost**: 1–3 kHz for clarity, 8–12 kHz for shimmer
- **Cut**: Remove lows below 80 Hz if not bass-heavy
- **Panning**: Center or slight stereo spread
- **Volume**: Adjust for balance with other layers
- **Reverb**: Medium decay (500–1,000 ms), lush or plate reverb

## 20. Deep Bass

- **Boost**: 50–80 Hz for sub-bass
- **Cut**: Muddiness around 200–300 Hz if needed
- **Panning**: Center
- **Volume**: Subtle but foundational
- **Compression**: Light, ratio 4:1, to control dynamics
- **Reverb**: Minimal; avoid reverb on bass

## 28. Vocal Samples
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: 100 Hz–8 kHz
- **Boost**: 3–6 kHz for clarity, 8–12 kHz for air

- **Panning**: Center or creatively panned for effect
- **Volume**: Adjust for desired emphasis
- **Reverb**: Medium to large decay (600–1,200 ms); consider delays for texture

## 29. Sample Chops
- **Fundamental Frequencies**: Variable (depends on original sample)
- **Boost**: Focus on clarity in the midrange (~1–4 kHz)
- **Cut**: Unnecessary lows below 80 Hz or highs above 10 kHz
- **Panning**: Wide stereo or creative placement
- **Volume**: Balance to sit well with other rhythmic or melodic elements
- **Reverb**: Short to medium decay, or creative effects
- **Additional Tips**: Use pitch correction, time-stretching, or effects to fit the sample into your mix
